Our Mission

The Junior Birding Society seeks to ignite a lifelong love of birds in elementary and middle school students and to spark a passion for scientific inquiry through engaging school outreach and events that celebrate birds and foster scientific curiosity.

Why Support Us?

The Junior Birding Society plays a key role in bridging the gap between the youth and birding. A study in the prestigious journal Nature titled, “Initial Involvement into birding: triggers, gender, and decade effects-a mixed-methods study” analyzed statistics regarding the ages of birders. Birders typically begin bird watching at the age of 23, with an average age of 46 among avid birders. Our goal is to make birding more accessible to elementary and middle school students by breaking down the walls that make birding so inaccessible: a steep learning curve and a lack of exposure. We plan to use resources such as Merlin and eBird to increase the accessibility and engagement in birding. Through our outreach programs and events, we are trying to give students the opportunity to see the world through the lens of both a birder and a scientist.



We want to inspire a whole new generation of birders and scientists.

Activities

We host a variety of different events to get as many people interested in birding as possible.


Aviary and Sanctuary Visits

Children can interact with birds, such as visiting bird sanctuaries or aviaries that allow hands-on experiences.

Seminars

Educational seminars about different bird species and their importance in the natural world, emphasizing conservation and ecological roles.

Bird Watching Trips

Guided trips where students can learn the basics of birdwatching, including identification techniques and observing behavior in their natural habitats.

Birding Workshops

Qualified birding experts teach students about the fundamentals of birding, including bird identification, habitat preservation, and conservation efforts.

Classroom Visits

Offer to bring birds directly to the classroom, providing interactive sessions where students can learn about birds up close.

Competitions

Competitions that involve activities like creating your own bird species, designing birdhouses, or solving bird-themed puzzles.

Become a Member!

It is completely free to become a member of the Junior Birding Society! By removing the financial barrier surrounding birding, we ensure that every student, regardless of their financial situation, has an equal opportunity to pursue birding. We believe that every student should have the chance to chase their passions. As a member, you can get access to exclusive birding opportunities and events. Join us as a member of the JBS!

About Us

Our team is made up of a group of enthusiastic birders and teens. We aim to inspire the next generation of students who are interested in establishing a connection with the natural world. The JBS team is a collection of student birders who are all connected by our love of birds and science. We hope to spread our message to the rest of the world!
